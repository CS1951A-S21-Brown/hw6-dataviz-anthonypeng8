Written Questions:

1. The first barplot answers the question on what the top 10 video games were all time. I decided to first rank them by North American Sales as that is our current location, then added an option to view the rank of the video games globally (global sales) for comparison. The top video games are at the top of graph with a longer bar to represent higher sales.
The pieplot answers the question overall (by global sales) on which genre is most popular. Also, it addresses the question of what are the top publishers for each genre. The pieplot in sorted order clock-wise represents the proportion/percentage of sales from the total in that genre. Furthermore, hovering over the slices will pop up a card telling what the top 3 publishers are for that genre.
The second barplot represents the genre sales broken down by region (NA, EU, JP, Other, Global). The barplot will rank the genres by sales with the highest and widest bar at the top. I included buttons to change the barplot based on which region to visualize for top genre.

4. I addressed the possiblity of colorblindness by not having color needed to correctly grasp the visualization. For example, from “A Comprehensive Guide to Accessible Data Visualization,” I made sure to add seperations in the slices of the piechart to ensure differences if the user cannot differentiate color. Users that might find it difficult to access the visualization would be visually impaired or unable to click on the actions to see more/change the visualization. For example, I have buttons for the user needs to click to see other parts of the visualization and hovering over the slices of the piechart to address the question of what are the top publishers for each genre. Steps to help overcome this, would be add other sensors other than vision (sound, touch, etc.) and to fully represent all the data without any action required to see them.
